# Tokenised funds research

## Scope and verification status

This note records the publicly discoverable on-chain token identifiers for the
25 tokenised funds in the July 2026 research list. It is a contract-discovery
reference, not an endorsement, eligibility determination, or investment
recommendation.

Checked on 2026-07-17. The principal data source is the relevant public
[RWA.xyz asset page](https://app.rwa.xyz/), which links each network deployment
to its explorer. Each address below links directly to that explorer. Where an
asset page exposes multiple contracts on the same network, all are included;
they may be separate share classes or token representations and must not be
treated as interchangeable without issuer confirmation.

`—` means that this research did not locate a public token identifier. It does
not mean that the fund has no on-chain representation.

## Corrections to the supplied table

- `iBENJI`, `BENJI`, and `gBENJI` are not one token. The public listings point
  to an Ethereum contract for iBENJI and separate Stellar assets for BENJI and
  gBENJI.
- JTRSY, JLTXX, USTB, CUMIU, OUSG, TBILL, VBILL, USTBL, BELIF, MONY, thBILL,
  ULTRA, FILQ, FDIT, mTBILL, CASHx, and DCP all have a public token identifier
  despite the supplied table leaving their address blank.
- SWEEP is Solana-first, rather than Ethereum-first. The issuer says that
  Ethereum and Stellar integrations are planned; a public Solana mint was not
  located.

## Production vault metadata coverage

Checked against the production vault metadata database on 2026-07-17 (39,490
resolved rows). A resolved row is counted as tracked; a discovery lead alone is
not. This distinction matters because every EVM contract in this list also has
a discovery lead, including the ones already classified.

Already tracked:

- **BUIDL** — two Ethereum rows, classified as **Securitize**, including the
  BUIDL and BUIDL-I token variants. Both carry the `tokenised_fund` flag.
- **JLTXX** — Ethereum row, classified as **Kinexys**.
- **VBILL** — Ethereum row, classified as **Securitize** and carrying the
  `tokenised_fund` flag.
- **mTBILL** — Ethereum, Base and Arbitrum rows, classified as **Midas**.

Tracked, but protocol unknown:

- **TBILL** — the Ethereum contract is present, but its resolved row has
  protocol `<unknown>` and empty name and symbol fields. It should be repaired
  or classified as OpenEden rather than added as a new vault.

The remaining funds have no resolved production row. This includes the
Stellar-only primary assets (BENJI and gBENJI), which cannot be ingested by the
current EVM vault pipeline without Stellar support.

### Reconciled findings

- **Known reusable implementations:** Centrifuge `Tranche` (JTRSY), Ondo's
  RWA token architecture (USDY and OUSG), Superstate's fund tokens (USTB),
  CMTA CMTAT with RuleEngine (CUMIU and BELIF), Spiko's token system (USTBL),
  Sygnum `SygToken` (FILQ), and Kinexys FACT (MONY).
- **Issuer-specific but source-verified systems:** USYC, iBENJI/BENJI, WTGXX,
  ULTRA, CASHx, FDIT and DCP. They expose ERC-20-like interfaces in most cases,
  but their transfer, issuance, compliance and upgrade controls differ
  materially; a generic ERC-4626 adapter would be incorrect.
- **Special cases:** thBILL has a bespoke multi-asset ERC-4626 extension;
  gBENJI is a Stellar classic asset; SWEEP has no publicly disclosed Solana
  mint or programme; DCP is ERC-1155 rather than an ERC-20 fund share.
- **Integration priority:** start with the public-source families and their
  product-specific contract registries. For the proprietary systems, the
  verified explorer source and issuer documentation are the only reliable
  integration references; deployment-specific GitHub links should not be
  invented from similarly named repositories.

## Operational cautions

- These are permissioned securities or fund interests in many cases. An
  ERC-20 address alone does not establish transfer eligibility, redemption
  rights, or investor qualification.
- Verify `name`, `symbol`, `decimals`, the active proxy implementation, and
  the issuer's allow-list requirements at the time of integration. Do not use
  a same-ticker token from a different chain or share class as a substitute.
- The [RWA.xyz API overview](https://app.rwa.xyz/platform-overview) describes
  its asset data as issuer-contributed, but the issuer and the relevant
  explorer remain the final source for an integration decision.
